A Note on Work Experience

My work history reflects the economic realities of being born working-class in the United States. As Matthew Desmond argues in his book Poverty, by America, the richest country in the world chooses to impoverish almost 40 million of its own citizens. The one thing it does not lack is resources. It could choose to end poverty within its borders if wanted to, but it chooses not to. Furthermore, the poverty rate of trans people in America is 33%, one of the highest poverty rates (if not the highest) of any group in America. Despite the progress on trans rights and cultural acceptance over the years, there is still rampant discrimination against trans people, as evidenced in the high rates of poverty and homelessness. While the U.S. frequently touts itself as a defender of liberty and freedom, for many trans Americans, that liberty and freedom has remained elusive, and what we have received instead is crushing economic instability, violence, discrimination, and a lack of bodily autonomy. To add insult to injury, in recent years, right-wing political movements have used trans people as political pawns in the U.S. and elsewhere. This will only serve to exacerbate the poverty and economic precarity of trans people the world over. And unfortunately, all too often, poverty--no matter who you are--simply begets more poverty, regardless of what people do or manage to accomplish.

This is why the results of the recent U.S. election are sadly not surprising. While I did not vote for Mr. Trump in any of the elections in which he ran because I do not condone or support his racism, misogyny, transphobia, xenophobia, and other bigotry, and I do not support his policies, I am not surprised that so many poor and working-class Americans did vote for him. When you are trapped in poverty or a low-income economic status and nothing you do ever creates a path out of that situation, it can lead many people to want to destroy the system they feel is rigged against them. As long as the current caste system in American and so much of the world is kept in place, and as long as poor and working-class people continue to be denied opportunities even when they earn them, I fear many of those people--my people--will continue to vote for the madmen, the strong man authoritarians who make (false) promises to burn things down. One way to combat this, however, is to stop hording opportunities only for the rich and middle-class and to start giving us poor and working-class people the opportunities we have earned and deserve.
